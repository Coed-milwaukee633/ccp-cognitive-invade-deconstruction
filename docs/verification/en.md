## Detailed Verification

### [1] King, G., Pan, J., & Roberts, M. E. (2017)

**Cited claim**: The Chinese government produces approximately 448 million social media posts per year.

- **Authors**: Gary King (Harvard University), Jennifer Pan (Stanford University), Margaret E. Roberts (UC San Diego)
- **Journal**: *American Political Science Review*, Vol. 111, No. 3, pp. 484–501
- **Core data**: The paper's abstract explicitly states "the government fabricates and posts about 448 million social media comments a year"
- **DOI**: `10.1017/S0003055417000144`

**Primary source**: [Cambridge Core](https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/american-political-science-review/article/how-the-chinese-government-fabricates-social-media-posts-for-strategic-distraction-not-engaged-argument/4662DB26E2685BAF1485F14369BD137C)

### [2] Google Threat Analysis Group / DRAGONBRIDGE (2024)

**Cited claim**: The DRAGONBRIDGE network had over 10,000 instances disrupted in Q1 2024 alone, with 175,000+ lifetime disruptions.

- **Source**: Google TAG blog post dated June 26, 2024, authored by Zak Butler
- **10,000+ instances (Q1 2024)**: Confirmed
- **175,000+ lifetime instances**: Confirmed
- **"50+ platforms"**: This figure is attributed to Meta [4], not Google TAG

**Primary source**: [Google TAG Blog](https://blog.google/threat-analysis-group/google-disrupted-dragonbridge-activity-q1-2024/)

### [4] Meta / Spamouflage CIB Reports (2019–2023)

**Cited claim**: Meta exposed the Spamouflage network involving 7,704 accounts and 954 pages, active across 50+ platforms.

- **7,704 accounts / 954 pages**: From Meta Q2 2023 Adversarial Threat Report. Also included 15 groups and 15 Instagram accounts
- **"Largest" description**: Meta's official language was "the largest known cross-platform covert influence operation in the world"
- **50+ platforms**: From Meta's August 2023 report (cited by Ben Nimmo)

**Primary source**: [Meta Announcement](https://about.fb.com/news/2023/08/raising-online-defenses/); [Meta Transparency Report](https://transparency.meta.com/integrity-reports-q2-2023/)

### [5] Hille, K. / Financial Times (2019) — Want Want Group

**Cited claim**: Financial Times investigation revealed China Times editorial operations were influenced by the TAO.

- **Author**: Kathrin Hille, FT's Greater China correspondent
- **Title**: "Taiwan primaries highlight fears over China's political influence," July 16, 2019
- **Content**: FT report was based on anonymous journalists alleging that TAO made "daily calls" influencing cross-strait coverage topics and angles. Want Want Group denied the allegations and filed (later dismissed) defamation suits

**Primary source**: [FT](https://www.ft.com/content/036b609a-a768-11e9-984c-fac8325aaa04); [RSF](https://rsf.org/en/taiwan-abusive-libel-suit-against-financial-times-correspondent)

### [7][8][9] Taiwan Espionage Prosecution Statistics

**Cited claim**: Espionage prosecutions have surged dramatically in recent years, with approximately two-thirds involving military personnel.

- **Source**: Taiwan's National Security Bureau report, January 12, 2025
- **Case counts**: 2021: 3 → 2022: 5 → 2023: 14 → 2024: 15
- **Individuals prosecuted**: 2021: 16 → 2022: 10 → 2023: 48 → 2024: 64
- **Military ratio**: Of 64 individuals in 2024: 28 active-duty + 15 retired = 43 (approximately 67%)

**Primary sources**: [Taipei Times](https://www.taipeitimes.com/News/front/archives/2025/01/13/2003830100); [Jamestown Foundation](https://jamestown.org/program/taiwan-exposes-more-prc-military-infiltration-cases/); [CEIAS](https://ceias.eu/a-threat-from-within-chinese-espionage-in-taiwan/); [CNN](https://edition.cnn.com/2025/01/13/china/taiwan-surge-suspected-chinese-espionage-hnk-intl)

### [10] Lin Te-wang / RFA (2023)

**Cited claim**: In October 2023, Taiwan People's Communist Party founder Lin Te-wang was indicted for violating the Anti-Infiltration Act.

- **Date**: Taipei District Prosecutors' Office filed charges on October 3, 2023
- **Legal basis**: Anti-Infiltration Act and Civil Servants Election and Recall Act
- **TAO connection**: Prosecutors alleged Lin had over a decade of contact with TAO Political Parties Bureau official Hu Chunguang
- **Note**: On June 9, 2025, the Taipei District Court ruled not guilty

**Primary source**: [RFA](https://www.rfa.org/english/news/china/taiwan-communist-infiltration-10042023041825.html); [Focus Taiwan](https://focustaiwan.tw/politics/202310030029)

## Methodology

1. **Verification scope**: Each citation was checked item-by-item for authors, year, title, publisher, and core data claims.
2. **Source hierarchy**: Primary sources (academic papers, official reports, platform announcements) were prioritized, with reliable news reporting used as secondary verification.
3. **Correction handling**: Errors discovered during verification were corrected directly in the document. Pre-correction versions are preserved in git history.
4. **Limitations**: Some paywalled articles (e.g., FT) were cross-verified through secondary reporting.
